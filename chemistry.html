Chemistry Topics

Organic Chemistry

Study of carbon-based compounds, their structure, properties, and reactions. Includes hydrocarbons, alcohols, and polymers.

Inorganic Chemistry

Focuses on inorganic compounds like metals, minerals, and organometallic compounds. Explores their behavior and applications.

Physical Chemistry

Examines how matter behaves on a molecular and atomic level and how chemical reactions occur using physics principles.

Analytical Chemistry

Involves the separation, identification, and quantification of chemical components in natural and artificial materials.

Biochemistry

Study of chemical processes within and relating to living organisms, including metabolism and molecular genetics.

Environmental Chemistry

Focuses on chemical phenomena in the environment, including pollution and biogeochemical cycles.

Amazing Chemistry Facts

There are about 94 elements that occur naturally on Earth, but the universe as a whole has about 118 known elements.

Water is one of the few substances that is less dense as a solid than as a liquid, which is why ice floats.

Lightning strikes produce ozone, which is why the air smells so fresh after a thunderstorm.

The only letter that doesn't appear on the periodic table is J. It was once believed that no element began with J.

A teaspoon of a neutron star would weigh about 6 billion tons due to its incredible density.

Helium is the only element that cannot be solidified by lowering the temperature at ordinary pressures.
